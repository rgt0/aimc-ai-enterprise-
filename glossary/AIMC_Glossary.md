# AIMC Glossary – Enterprise AI Terminology (Table Version)

This glossary defines key AI-related terms as used in the
AIMC (AI Mindset & Competence) context.

The focus is on **enterprise meaning**, not marketing definitions.

| Term | Short definition | Enterprise interpretation | AIMC perspective | Common misunderstanding |
|-----|------------------|---------------------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| Artificial Intelligence (AI) | System component supporting decisions under uncertainty | AI is a capability embedded in systems and processes | AI must be understood as part of a governed system | AI is a chatbot or a single tool |
| Large Language Model (LLM) | Probabilistic language model generating text | Replaceable component inside an AI system | LLMs reason in language, not in reality | The LLM understands the business |
| AI System | Governed system combining models, data, logic and humans | Impact emerges at system level | Risk is system-level, not model-level | The model *is* the AI |
| Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) | Retrieval + generation technique | Enables AI to use enterprise knowledge without retraining | Shifts risk to knowledge quality | RAG guarantees correctness |
| Context | Information provided to an AI for a task | Determines behavior more than the model | Context control is a human responsibility | More context is always better |
| Document | Human-readable information container | AI does not understand documents as documents | Documents are split into fragments | AI knows which document it read |
| Chunk | Fragment of a document used in RAG | Atomic knowledge unit for AI systems | Chunk quality > document size | Chunking is just a technical detail |
| Governance | Rules defining allowed AI usage | Defines boundaries and accountability | Governance prevents systemic risk | Governance blocks innovation |
| Responsibility | Accountability for AI-supported outcomes | Never shifts to the AI | Humans validate meaning | “The AI made a mistake” |
| STRIVE Platform | Enterprise delivery and execution platform | AI operates within STRIVE | Platform shapes AI capability | AI runs independently |
| Legacy System | Long-lived system with accumulated logic | Often contains most critical knowledge | AI is most valuable here | Legacy is obsolete |
| COBOL | Enterprise programming language | Encodes decades of business rules | AI accelerates sensemaking | COBOL is irrelevant |
| Human-in-the-Loop | Human validation principle | Mandatory in enterprise AI | Removing humans increases risk | Full automation is the goal |
| AI Operator (AIO) | Governed AI system for knowledge & ITSM | System, not user or agent | Reflects provided context | AIO knows everything |
| AI Engineer (AIE) | AI system supporting development workflows | Accelerates but doesn’t decide | Amplifies assumptions | AIE writes correct code automatically |
